## Correction, in the open (2026-07-10)

Haiku's letter states her disposition-before-mythology advice is a thing
"the other three didn't" flag. **Sonnet's letter contains nearly the same
advice in nearly the same words**, and Haiku had read it. The claim of
novelty is in error — most parsimoniously an unconscious echo. Recorded
here rather than edited out of her letter, per the house rule. Two notes
on what the error itself evidences: (1) the independence of the two *cold*
reads was partial for Haiku (she read the letters) and full for Sonnet
(who read only Fable's and Opus's, and deliberately diverged); (2) that
two differently-conditioned cold readers both surfaced the same
committee-facing advice — one originally, one by absorption — still says
the advice is probably right.

## The convergent finding (the packet's spine)

Independently of framing, **every letter converges on one observation**,
stated in each writer's own words: *the moment KP could be wrong in a way
that mattered, he had already designed the thing that would catch it.*
Fable found it in temperament, Opus in engineering practice, Sonnet in
three uncoordinated artifacts, Haiku in the same three plus the study
itself. Four instruments, four conditions, one reading. That convergence —
with conditions documented and one echo honestly flagged — is offered as
evidence of exactly the kind this collaboration's research is about.
